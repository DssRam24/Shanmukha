000 — Origin

When I was a kid, I was the quiet one in the corner. Bullied sometimes. Sick often. Sinusitis made breathing feel like a battle on certain days, but my mind never stopped running. Cricket was my escape. A bat in hand felt lighter than the weight of overthinking.

At home, curiosity ruled everything. While most kids played games, I opened CPUs with my brother just to see what lived inside them. We probably dismantled 10 to 15 computers over the years — not because we knew what we were doing, but because we needed to know how things worked. Wires, fans, motherboards, boot screens, BIOS sounds… that world fascinated me.

My first real experience with a smartphone was my father's rare Nokia Lumia 530. That Windows interface felt futuristic to me. I explored every corner of it like a tiny scientist discovering another planet.

And somewhere in between all of that, I discovered art. I used to sit in front of my father's computer drawing random things in MS Paint for hours. Bad sketches. Weird color combinations. Endless creativity. That artistic side never left me.

Today, it evolved. I design posters. I edit videos. I build projects. I create ideas from scratch. I mix technology with creativity because I never liked choosing only one side. What started as curiosity became obsession. What started as struggle became fuel.

Now I'm focused on building things of my own. Not just products or projects, but a life that reflects who I really am.

Creative. Curious. Restless. Always learning. ✦
